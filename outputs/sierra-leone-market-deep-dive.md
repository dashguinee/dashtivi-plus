### 1.6 Disposable Income Reality

For an average formal sector worker earning ~$75/month:
- Food & basics: ~$40 (53%)
- Transport: ~$10 (13%)
- Rent contribution: ~$10-15 (15%)
- Phone/airtime: ~$3-5 (5%)
- **Everything else (including entertainment): ~$5-10/month**

For minimum wage workers ($35/month), entertainment spending is measured in cents, not dollars.

> **Critical pricing insight:** DashTivi+ must price at **$1-2/month maximum** to be viable for mass adoption. Daily passes at $0.10-0.25 are even more realistic. Anything above $3/month is premium/diaspora territory.

### 2.7 Network Coverage Reality

- Freetown: Good (both Orange and Africell)

### 2.8 Electricity — The Hidden Killer

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Grid connection rate** | ~29.4% nationwide |
| **Rural electricity access** | ~5% |
| **Power generation** | Severely inadequate |
| **Blackout frequency** | Daily, multi-hour outages common |

Sierra Leone's electricity situation is dire. In 2025, the country paid $90 million to Karpowership for emergency power in just 6 months. The Bumbuna hydropower plant generates 30-40MW in wet season but drops to 10-18MW in dry season, causing severe Feb-April blackouts.

> **Impact on DashTivi+:** Offline downloads are not optional — they are ESSENTIAL. Users need to download content when they have power and data, then watch offline. The app must support background downloads and aggressive caching.

## 3. ENTERTAINMENT CONSUMPTION

### 3.1 What Sierra Leoneans Watch Now

**Tier 1: Radio (King of Media)**
- 85% of population has radio access
- 72% listen daily
- Peak times: 6-10am and 6-10pm
- Most trusted media source
- BBC World Service and Capital Radio are most popular
- Only media reaching rural populations effectively

**Tier 2: Television (Urban phenomenon)**
- Free-to-air: SLBC (government), AYV TV (Channel 33 & 34), Star TV
- Pay TV: DStv (dominant), StarTimes (growing)
- TV watching is largely a Freetown phenomenon
- Outside major cities, very few people watch TV
- 26 local TV stations exist but many operate irregularly

**Tier 3: Social Media & YouTube**
- Facebook: 1.15 million users (dominant platform)
- YouTube: Growing, especially for music and Nollywood
- WhatsApp: Widely used for messaging and group sharing
- TikTok: Fast-growing, especially among youth
- Instagram: 109,000 users
- Twitter/X: Declining (39,400 users, -14.7% YoY)

**Tier 4: Physical Media & Piracy**
- USB drives and SD cards loaded with movies still circulate
- Telegram channels share pirated Nollywood and Hollywood content
- Market stalls sell compressed DVDs (5-20 films per disc for ~$4)
- Movies compressed and shared via Bluetooth/file transfer between phones

### 3.2 DStv in Sierra Leone

DStv is the incumbent pay-TV champion. Here are the current packages:

| Package | Monthly Price | Channels | Key Content |
|---------|--------------|----------|-------------|
| **Premium** | $52.17 | 140+ (40+ HD) | Full SuperSport (16 channels), blockbusters |
| **Compact Plus** | $30.43 | 130+ (30+ HD) | Champions League, UFC, NBA |
| **Compact** | $19.13 | 95+ (25 HD) | Premier League, FA Cup, WWE |
| **Family** | $11.30 | 85+ (10+ HD) | La Liga, Serie A, family entertainment |
| **Access** | $6.09 | 70+ (10+ HD) | Africa Magic, some sports, music |

**Payment methods:**
- Cash to local DStv agents (most common)
- Orange Money (dial #144#)
- Africell Money (Afrimoney)
- Online via MyDStv

**DStv's weaknesses in Sierra Leone:**
- Requires decoder purchase (~$50-100 upfront)
- Requires satellite dish installation
- Even Access package at $6.09/month is expensive relative to income
- No mobile streaming option for most users
- Blackouts mean decoder is useless without power
- No local Sierra Leonean content

**Estimated DStv subscriber base in SL:** Low thousands (no public data, but given pricing vs. income, penetration is very limited — likely <50,000 households maximum).

### 3.3 StarTimes in Sierra Leone

StarTimes operates in Sierra Leone with cheaper alternatives to DStv:
- Chinese-backed, focused on affordability
- Decoder prices: ~$10-15 (much cheaper than DStv)
- Monthly packages from ~$2-3 (basic) to ~$10-15 (premium)
- DTT (terrestrial) and DTH (satellite) options
- Carries Nollywood content, Chinese dramas, and some sports

> **StarTimes is DashTivi+'s closest competitor in positioning** — both target the mass market with affordability. But StarTimes still requires hardware purchase and physical installation.

### 3.5 Football — The National Obsession

Football is not just a sport in Sierra Leone; it's the cultural heartbeat.

**How they watch European football:**
- DStv subscribers watch via SuperSport channels (Premier League, Champions League, La Liga, Serie A)
- SuperSport app on mobile (requires DStv subscription)
- Free-to-air: One EPL match live every Saturday via Infront deal
- **Viewing centers/bars:** Communal spaces where people gather to watch matches on a single TV with DStv — this is how MOST fans experience live football
- Illegal IPTV streams on phones (growing rapidly)
- Highlight clips on YouTube, TikTok, social media

**Club loyalties:**
- **Arsenal** — Massive following, organized supporters club with 300+ members at La Paz Restaurant, Freetown
- **Manchester City** — One of the largest Man City fan clubs outside the UK
- **Chelsea, Manchester United, Liverpool** — All have strong followings
- Local clubs compete for attention but European football dominates

**National team:**
- Leone Stars — source of national pride
- AFCON participation drives massive viewership

> **DashTivi+ football strategy:** Live football is the single most powerful content driver. Even highlights, analysis shows, and fan community features around EPL would generate significant engagement. If live sports rights are feasible, they would be the ultimate growth hack.

### 3.7 Nollywood Consumption

### 4.2 Mobile Money Operators

**Orange Money:**
- Market leader in mobile money (tied to Orange's strong data position)
- Services: deposits, withdrawals, transfers, bill payments, airtime purchase
- Disbursed $117M+ to rural communities via World Bank/WHO programs (2025)
- DStv payments integrated (dial #144#)
- Agent network across Freetown and major cities

**Africell Afrimoney:**
- ~372,000+ registered users, 3,584+ agents (growing)
- Services: cash in/out, transfers, bill pay, airtime
- New agent mobile app launched recently
- Minimum transaction: 1 Leone
- Daily limits apply

**Other services:**
- Qodoo Mobile Money
- Sierratel Money
- Sierra Leone Commercial Bank digital services

### 4.3 National Payment Infrastructure (2023-2025)

Major developments:
- **National Payment Switch** launched May 2023 — interconnects 6 commercial banks
- Bank card interoperability implemented
- Point-of-sale terminal interoperability added
- **Instant Payment Service** launched to facilitate loans and cashless transactions
- UBA opened 250,000+ new accounts and issued 35,740 cards in 2024

### 4.4 How People Pay for Entertainment Today

**DStv payments:**
1. Cash to local DStv agents (most popular)
2. Orange Money via USSD (#144# > Make Payment > DSTV)
3. Afrimoney
4. Online via MyDStv (tiny minority)

**Airtime/data purchases:**
- Street vendors selling scratch cards (dominant)
- Mobile money (Orange Money, Afrimoney)
- Agent shops

**Key insight: The scratch card model works.** People walk to a vendor, pay NLe 5-20 in cash, get an activation code. This is frictionless and trusted.

> **DashTivi+ payment strategy must include:**
> 1. **Orange Money integration** (USSD-based, no app needed)
> 2. **Afrimoney integration**
> 3. **Scratch card/voucher codes** sold at airtime vendor points
> 4. **Daily/weekly micro-pricing** (not just monthly)
> 5. **Cash agent network** — DStv proves this works

### 4.5 Price Points That Work

- **Daily data bundle:** NLe 0.40-5.20 ($0.02-0.22) — people buy daily
- **Weekly data bundle:** NLe 2-40 ($0.08-1.67)

- **DStv cheapest:** $6.09/month — perceived as expensive by most
- **StarTimes cheapest:** ~$2-3/month

> **There is NO local/affordable streaming platform specifically designed for Sierra Leone's mass market.** This is the gap DashTivi+ fills.

### 5.2 The Piracy Landscape

**How piracy works in Sierra Leone:**
1. **Physical media:** USB drives and SD cards loaded at market stalls with 20-50 movies for ~NLe 50-100 ($2-4)
2. **Telegram channels:** 40,000+ member groups sharing Nollywood, Hollywood content
3. **Bluetooth/WiFi sharing:** Phone-to-phone transfer of movie files
4. **Illegal IPTV streams:** Accessed via free apps for live football
5. **YouTube:** Many Nollywood channels upload full movies for free (grey area)

**Why people pirate:**
- **Price:** Legal options too expensive ($6+ monthly)
- **Availability:** Content they want isn't on legal platforms
- **Habit:** Piracy infrastructure well-established
- **No payment method:** Can't pay for Netflix even if they wanted to
- **Data savings:** Downloaded files can be watched offline for free

> **DashTivi+ anti-piracy strategy:** Don't fight piracy with DRM. Beat it with convenience, price, and local content. If DashTivi+ costs NLe 1/day ($0.04) and has content pirate channels don't (local shows, live sports, exclusive music), people will pay.

### 5.3 What's Missing (Market Gaps)

1. **Affordable mobile-first streaming** — No platform is priced for the mass market
2. **Local Sierra Leonean content** — No one is aggregating Salone music, comedy, reality TV
3. **Krio-language entertainment** — Massive demand, zero supply from global platforms
4. **Football fan experience** — No dedicated, affordable football streaming for SL fans
5. **Offline-first design** — No streaming app is designed for intermittent power/connectivity
6. **USSD-accessible payment** — No streaming service lets you subscribe via USSD code
7. **Low-data streaming** — No platform optimizes for the 1-5GB/month reality
8. **Community features** — No platform leverages WhatsApp-group culture for content sharing
9. **Daily micro-pricing** — Only telcos offer daily pricing; content services don't

## 6. CULTURAL TONE & MARKETING INTELLIGENCE

### 6.1 The Krio Language

Krio is the lingua franca spoken by **96% of the population**. It's an English-based creole and the de facto national language that unites all ethnic groups. Any brand that doesn't speak Krio is an outsider.

**Essential Krio phrases for DashTivi+ marketing:**

| Krio | English | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| Kusheh / Kusheh-o | Hello | Universal greeting |
| Aw di bodi? | How are you? | Warm greeting |
| A fayn fayn | I'm fine | Response |
| Teki / Teki am | Watch / Watch it | For content prompts |
| Wi go si bak | See you later | Session end |
| Duya | Please | Polite request |
| Wetin de? | What's happening? | Casual check-in |

## 7. SUCCESSFUL ANALOGUES & LESSONS

### 7.1 iROKOtv (Nigeria) — THE CAUTIONARY TALE

**What happened:** iROKOtv, the "Netflix of Africa," burned through $100 million trying to build a paid streaming service in Nigeria before exiting in 2023.

**Key lessons for DashTivi+:**

| Lesson | Detail | DashTivi+ Response |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Subscription model failed** | Nigerians ($2,000 GDP/capita) couldn't sustain $5/month | Price at $0.04-1.04/month instead |
| **Currency devaluation kills** | 3,000 Naira went from $18 to $6.60 | Price in NLe, not USD; adjust monthly |
| **Data costs too high** | 3GB costs more than a day's wage | Zero-rated data bundles with telcos |
| **Payment failures** | 40% digital payment failure rate in Nigeria | USSD + scratch cards + cash agents |
| **Free content won** | YouTube Nollywood channels killed paid demand | Include free tier with ads |
| **Over-raised capital** | $35M raised distorted urgency | Stay lean, prove unit economics early |
| **Content production wins** | ROK Studios (production arm) was profitable | Focus on content creation, not just distribution |
| **Diaspora vs. local** | Diaspora was the real paying customer | Build diaspora pricing tier separately |

> **The single most important lesson:** Jason Njoku himself said they could have reached the same conclusions with $5-10M instead of $100M. DashTivi+ must validate before scaling.

### 7.2 Showmax (Pan-Africa) — THE SMART ADAPTOR

**What they did right:**
- Mobile-first: First African streamer with mobile downloads for offline viewing
- First to launch mobile-only plan (R39/month in SA, ~$2)
- Data Saver mode: 60MB/hour (lowest in industry) — 3.24MB/minute
- Three bandwidth caps (60MB, 240MB, 330MB, 1.6GB per hour)
- Local content investment: Showmax originals in local languages
- Payment flexibility: Mobile money, vouchers, carrier billing

**What went wrong:**
- Still lost $265 million despite these innovations
- Couldn't overcome Netflix brand recognition
- MultiChoice integration was messy
- Eventually sold to Canal+ (shutting down in 2026)

**Lessons for DashTivi+:**
- Data optimization is table stakes, not differentiator
- Local content is the real moat — Showmax's originals drove growth
- Payment infrastructure > content library in importance
- Being acquired by a bigger player is a valid exit

### 7.3 StarTimes (Pan-Africa) — THE MASS MARKET MACHINE

**Their playbook:**
- Chinese-backed, relentless affordability focus
- Decoder prices slashed repeatedly (as low as $5-10 in some markets)
- Packages from $2-3/month at entry level
- DTT (terrestrial) doesn't need satellite dish — easier installation
- Active in 37+ African countries
- Mixes Chinese content (dramas, documentaries) with Nollywood and sports

**Why StarTimes matters for DashTivi+:**
- Proves the mass market will pay ~$2-3/month for entertainment
- Hardware requirement (decoder) is their main limitation
- DashTivi+ can undercut by being phone-only (no hardware purchase)
- StarTimes' Housemates Salone deal shows they invest in local content partnerships

### 7.4 Boomplay (Music Streaming) — THE AFRICAN GROWTH MODEL

**Their playbook:**
- 90M+ active users across Africa
- Dual model: ad-supported free tier + Premium at $1.50/month
- Focus on African artists first, global catalog second
- Licensing deals with Universal, Warner, Sony, TikTok
- Mobile-first, offline download capable
- Pre-installed on Transsion phones (Tecno, Infinix, Itel) — which dominate Sierra Leone

> **Critical insight:** Boomplay comes PRE-INSTALLED on Tecno, Infinix, and Itel phones. These three brands account for ~40% of Sierra Leone's mobile market. DashTivi+ should pursue similar pre-installation deals with Transsion.

### 7.5 M-Pesa Agent Model (Kenya) — THE DISTRIBUTION FRAMEWORK

**Key elements applicable to DashTivi+:**
- **Super-agent model:** A few large agents sell to sub-agents who serve customers. Keeps operations lean.
- **Commission incentive:** Agents earn 1-2% per transaction. DashTivi+ agents could earn 15-20% per subscription sold.
- **Ubiquity:** 298,890 active agent outlets in Kenya. Sierra Leone has 3,500+ Afrimoney agents already — leverage them.
- **Trust through proximity:** Physical agents build trust that digital-only platforms can't.

**Application to DashTivi+:**
- Partner with existing Afrimoney/Orange Money agents
- Agents sell DashTivi+ scratch cards or activate subscriptions
- Agents earn commission on each activation
- Use agent network for customer support too
- Physical branding at agent points creates visibility

### 7.6 The MultiChoice/Canal+ Playbook (2025-2026)

Canal+ acquired MultiChoice and is now:
- Cutting costs aggressively
- Hiring 1,000+ sales staff across Africa
- Reducing decoder prices by 30-40%
- Lowering entry barriers for new customers
- Replacing Showmax with Canal+ streaming

**This means:**
- The pay-TV market is consolidating into a Canal+ monopoly
- Entry-level pricing will drop, but not to micro-payment levels
- An opportunity exists for nimble, mobile-first alternatives
- Canal+ will focus on premium; DashTivi+ can own the mass market
