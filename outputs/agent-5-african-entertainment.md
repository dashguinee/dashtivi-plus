## KEY FINDINGS & INSIGHTS

### 1. Primary Sources Are Reliable
- **iptv-org/iptv**: Official database, community-maintained, well-documented
- **Free-TV/IPTV**: Community-driven, frequent updates
- **IPTV Cat**: Daily verification of streams (best for dead-link avoidance)

### 2. Francophone Africa Dominance
Guinea, Senegal, Ivory Coast, and other French-speaking West African nations have the most available streams due to:
- French language popularity
- Canal+ distribution network (Gulli, Novelas TV, etc.)
- Strong French media presence (France 24, TV5 Monde)

### 3. Category Distribution
- **News**: France 24, Africa 24, RTGs, RTIs
- **Music**: Trace networks (Urban, Mziki, Gospel, Tropical)
- **Entertainment**: Africable, A+, Nollywood, ROK TV, local broadcasters
- **Kids**: Gulli Africa (official Canal+ channel)
- **Telenovelas**: Novelas TV (Canal+ West Africa)

### 4. Stream Quality Variations
- Most streams available in HD (720p-1080p)
- Some regional variations (SD fallbacks)
- Quality depends on source network's broadcast standard

### 5. Geographic Access
- Some streams (RTI, RTS) may have geo-restrictions
- VPN/proxy re-positioning may be required
- Most Pan-African channels (Trace, France 24) have global access

## CHALLENGES & LIMITATIONS

1. **URL Volatility**: Stream URLs change periodically as sources move CDNs or deprecate endpoints
2. **Geo-blocking**: Some national broadcasters (RTI, RTS) restrict non-regional access
3. **Quality Variability**: Free IPTV quality depends on broadcaster's streaming infrastructure
4. **Legal Gray Area**: Free IPTV exists in legal ambiguity (hosts claim public links only, but underlying content may lack proper licensing)
