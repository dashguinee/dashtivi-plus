## 1. BRAND VOICE GUIDE

### Who Is DashTivi+ Talking To?

Young adults, 18-35, in Freetown. They watch football at bars, at home, on their phones. They love EPL (Arsenal, Chelsea, Man United, Liverpool — in that order of noise level). They watch Nollywood and Hollywood. They scroll Facebook and WhatsApp Status all day. They know DStv is expensive. They know YouTube buffers. They want something that just WORKS on their phone without breaking their pocket.

### The Voice: "Your Sharp Padi"

DashTivi+ speaks like that one friend who always knows what's happening — which match is on, what new movie dropped, where the vibes are. Not a corporation. Not a teacher. Not trying to impress. Just... sharp. Confident. Plugged in.

**If DashTivi+ was a person:**
- 25 years old
- Lives in Freetown, knows the city inside out
- Always has their phone charged
- First to know match lineups, first to share highlights
- Generous — shares codes, invites friends, looks out for their people
- Speaks English with natural Krio mixed in — not forced, not performative

### Language Rules

**The mix:** Primarily English with Krio words and phrases dropped in naturally. NOT full Krio (too hard to read on screen). NOT full formal English (too stiff, feels foreign).

**Krio that works in marketing:**

| Krio | Meaning | Use it for |
|------|---------|------------|
| "How di bodi?" | How are you? / How's it going? | Greetings, re-engagement |
| "Na so!" | That's it! / Exactly! | Agreement, confirmation |
| "Swit!" / "E swit!" | Sweet! / It's sweet! | Describing content, experience |
| "Boku" | Plenty / A lot | Channel count, content volume |
| "Padi" / "Paddy" | Friend / Buddy | Referral, casual address |
| "Lef dat!" | Forget that! / Leave it! | Dismissing competition |
| "Nor to lie" | No lie / For real | Emphasis, authenticity |
| "Waka" | Walk / Go | Call to action ("waka go tivi.dasuperhub.com") |
| "Sabi" | Know / Understand | "You sabi wetin ah mean" |
| "Kolneh" | Corner / Spot | "Every kolneh for Freetown" |
| "Wetin?" | What? | Hooks, curiosity |
| "Aw di go?" | How's it going? | Check-in messages |
| "Enjoyment no get limit" | Fun has no limit | Tagline energy |
| "Na we get am" | We own it / It's ours | Pride, ownership |
| "Ol man pikin" | Everyone / Everybody | Inclusive messaging |
| "Blow!" | Amazing / Impressive | Reaction to content |
| "Shake bodi" | Get excited / Celebrate | Match day energy |
| "Make we go!" | Let's go! | CTA energy |

**Tone spectrum:**

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Match day promo | HYPE, loud, exclamation marks | "TONIGHT! Chelsea vs Arsenal! Open DASH, sit back, ENJOY! 🔥" |
| New user welcome | Warm, helpful, clear | "Welcome to DASH fam. Everything set up for you. Any problem, just message us." |
| Price comparison | Confident, factual, a little cheeky | "DStv Premium? $52 a month. DASH? Less than $6. Same Premier League. You do the maths." |
| Re-engagement | Casual, no pressure | "Aw di go? Long time we nor see you for DASH. New movies just drop. Come check am." |
| Agent recruitment | Professional but exciting | "You sabi sell? You get phone? DASH dey look for agents in Freetown. Good money, real product." |

### What to NEVER Say

| Never Say | Why | Say Instead |
|-----------|-----|-------------|
| "Subscribe to our premium streaming platform" | Sounds like a press release | "Get DASH on your phone" |
| "We leverage cutting-edge technology" | Nobody talks like that | "E dey work smooth on any phone" |
| "Dear valued customer" | Corporate, cold, fake | "Hey!" or just start talking |
| "Kindly" | Too formal for this market | "Just" or nothing |
| "Click below to learn more" | Email voice, not WhatsApp voice | "Check am here: [link]" |
| "Content library" | Nobody calls it that | "Movies, series, channels" |
| "Buffering issues" | Don't bring up problems | "Smooth like butter" |
| "Affordable pricing" | Sounds like you're apologizing for being cheap | Just state the price confidently |
| "Best in class" | Generic corporate garbage | Show, don't tell — name the channels |
| Any price in GNF only | You're in Sierra Leone | Always show SLE or USD |

## 5. AGENT SCRIPTS

### The 30-Second Pitch

> "Aw di go! Let me ask you quick — you like football? You dey watch Premier League?
>
> Check this out. [Pull out phone, open DASH, show a live channel]
>
> This na DashTivi+. Live TV, straight to your phone. Premier League, Champions League, movies, everything. Over 8,000 channels.
>
> No decoder. No satellite dish. Just your phone browser. And the price? Under $6 a month. Less than one plate of food at Lumley Beach.
>
> You want me set you up? Takes 2 minutes."

### How to Demo the App

**Step by step — practice this until it's smooth:**

1. **Open DASH on YOUR phone first** (always have it ready, already logged in)
2. **Show the Sports section** — scroll through football channels. If a match is live, show it playing. If no match, show a sports news channel.
3. **Show it's LIVE** — point out "this na live right now, not recording"
4. **Switch to Movies** — show the movie library, tap a Nollywood or Hollywood poster
5. **Show the speed** — "See how fast e dey load? No buffering."
6. **Show the Kids section** (if they have kids) — "Your pikin dem go love this part"
7. **Turn the phone to them** — let them tap around for 30 seconds. Let them FEEL it.
8. **Close with:** "Na the same thing you go get for your own phone. Want me set you up?"

**Demo tips:**
- ALWAYS have DASH open before you approach someone. Don't fumble with loading.
- If your connection is slow, demo on WiFi first. Know your strong signal spots.
- Show football FIRST. Everything else is bonus.
- Let them hold the phone. People trust what they touch.

### Handling Objections

**"I already have DStv."**

> "Nor wahala! DStv na good product. But check this — how much you dey pay? [Wait for answer]
>
> DASH give you same Premier League, same Champions League, PLUS Nollywood, Bollywood, kids channels, over 8,000 channels total. All for under $6 a month.
>
> And look — you nor need to cancel DStv. Try DASH for one month. If you like am, you fit decide later. But I guarantee say once you see the price difference, you go know wetin make sense.
>
> Plus — DASH dey work for your phone. So even when you nor dey home, you still dey watch. DStv dey only work for house."

**"I can watch on YouTube."**

> "YouTube good for highlights, I agree. But when the match dey live, YouTube nor get am. You go dey see the goals 20 minutes late while everybody else dey celebrate already.
>
> With DASH, you dey watch LIVE. Same time. No spoilers. And e nor be only football — movies, series, everything. YouTube nor get those full movies and series.
>
> Plus YouTube eat your data like crazy with all dem ads. DASH? No ads. Clean."

**"Too expensive." / "I nor get money for that."**

> "I hear you. But make we do the maths. Under $6 a month — that's less than what you go spend on transport to go watch match at bar. Less than two pack of cigarettes. Less than one big popcorn.
>
> And you get 8,000 channels. 24 hours a day. Every day for the month.
>
> If you spread am out, na less than 20 cents a day. For LIVE Premier League on your phone. That nor be expensive — that na steal. 😄"

**"My phone too old" / "My phone nor go fit."**

> "If your phone get browser and internet, DASH go work. Android, iPhone, even Tecno, Infinix, itel — all of dem. E nor be big app wey take space. Na just website wey you open. Try am — if e work, you go see am right now. If e nor work, no charge."

**"I go think about it."**

> "Na fair. But let me do this — let me send you the link for WhatsApp. When you ready, just open am. And if you sign up this week, I go make sure you get [bonus/extra time].
>
